# IPFS-Sats: A Jurisdictional Protocol for Wealth-Based Information Persistence

## Purpose

**IPFS-Sats is a protocol that makes information persistence enforceable through wealth, without storing content on the Bitcoin blockchain.**

It exists to resolve a coordination failure created when Bitcoin’s Economic field is asked to carry Cultural persistence. Rather than restricting Bitcoin or fragmenting consensus, IPFS-Sats externalizes information storage while anchoring its persistence guarantees to Bitcoin’s wealth-based security.

Bitcoin remains a **capital management system**.  
IPFS-Sats operates as a **jurisdictional layer** that reshapes the cost landscape of information persistence.

---

## Coordination Geometry Anchor

In coordination geometry, fields do not replace one another; they **reshape the cost surfaces** within which behavior optimizes.

- **Economic Field** → allocates capital via scarcity and price
    
- **Jurisdictional Field** → enforces commitments by altering the cost of non-compliance
    

Bitcoin is maximally effective when confined to the Economic field. When it subsidizes non-economic data persistence (e.g. via discounted witness space), Cultural use begins to optimize _inside_ the Economic substrate, creating purpose drift.

IPFS-Sats corrects this by **re-externalizing persistence into the Jurisdictional field**, while remaining anchored to Bitcoin as the root of wealth and time.

---

## Core Insight

> **Persistence should be expensive to abandon, not expensive to create.**

IPFS by itself enables content addressing and routing, but does not enforce long-term availability. Data disappears when it is no longer convenient to host.

IPFS-Sats introduces a jurisdictional gradient:

- Storing data is voluntary
    
- Abandoning persistence carries an economic consequence
    
- Compliance emerges because it is the lower-cost path
    

No commands.  
No censorship.  
Only cost geometry.

---

## What IPFS-Sats Is

IPFS-Sats is a **Bitcoin-anchored commitment protocol** with three minimal functions:

1. **Commitment Anchoring**
    
    - Content is addressed by cryptographic hash (e.g. IPFS CID)
        
    - A compact commitment (hash / root / pointer) is anchored to Bitcoin using OP_RETURN
        
    - Bitcoin serves as the immutable provenance record of _what_ is being committed to and _when_
        
2. **Wealth-Based Persistence Pressure**
    
    - Satoshis are bonded to the commitment over time
        
    - Continued persistence is cheaper than abandonment
        
    - Failure to maintain availability converts into economic loss or forfeiture
        
3. **Jurisdictional Enforcement Without Consensus Change**
    
    - Bitcoin consensus rules are untouched
        
    - No transaction filtering
        
    - No content judgment
        
    - Enforcement occurs entirely through incentive gradients at the application layer
        

IPFS-Sats does not tell anyone what data to store.  
It makes promises expensive to break.

---

## What IPFS-Sats Is Not

- ❌ Not on-chain storage
    
- ❌ Not a Bitcoin fork, soft fork, or policy filter
    
- ❌ Not a content platform
    
- ❌ Not a cultural or expressive layer
    
- ❌ Not a replacement for IPFS
    

IPFS-Sats does **not** try to make Bitcoin the archive.  
It makes Bitcoin the **court of record**.

---

## Violations as Measurements

In jurisdictional systems, violations are not failures; they are **measurements of gradient strength**.

- Missing data
    
- Under-replication
    
- Slow retrieval
    
- Abandoned commitments
    

These outcomes reveal:

- Where persistence is underpriced
    
- Where enforcement is too permeable
    
- Where economic incentives overpower jurisdictional cost
    

IPFS-Sats is designed to adjust through observation, not decree.

---

## Why This Preserves Bitcoin’s Integrity

IPFS-Sats resolves the tension created by SegWit and Taproot **without undoing them**.

- Optional on-chain data remains valid
    
- No consensus splits
    
- No moral arbitration
    
- No cultural conflict imported into the Economic field
    

Instead, IPFS-Sats makes off-chain persistence:

- Cheaper
    
- Stronger
    
- More expressive
    
- More economically honest
    

On-chain storage is not prohibited — it simply becomes irrational.

---

## One-Line Summary

> **IPFS-Sats is a jurisdictional protocol that binds information persistence to wealth, allowing Bitcoin to remain a pure economic substrate while making durable off-chain storage the lowest-cost path.**
