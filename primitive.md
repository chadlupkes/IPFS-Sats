# IPFS-Sats: A Jurisdictional Protocol for Wealth-Based Information Persistence

## Purpose

**IPFS-Sats is a protocol that makes information persistence enforceable through wealth, without storing content on the Bitcoin blockchain.**

It exists to resolve a coordination failure created when Bitcoin's Economic field is asked to carry Cultural persistence. Rather than restricting Bitcoin or fragmenting consensus, IPFS-Sats externalizes information storage while anchoring its persistence guarantees to Bitcoin's wealth-based security.

Bitcoin remains a **capital management system**.
IPFS-Sats operates as a **jurisdictional layer** that reshapes the cost landscape of information persistence.

---

## Coordination Geometry Anchor

In coordination geometry, fields do not replace one another; they **reshape the cost surfaces** within which behavior optimizes.

- **Economic Field** → allocates capital via scarcity and price
- **Jurisdictional Field** → enforces commitments by altering the cost of non-compliance

Bitcoin is maximally effective when confined to the Economic field. When it subsidizes non-economic data persistence — via discounted witness space or other mechanisms — Cultural use begins to optimize inside the Economic substrate, creating purpose drift.

IPFS-Sats corrects this by **re-externalizing persistence into the Jurisdictional field**, while remaining anchored to Bitcoin as the root of wealth and time.

---

## Core Insight

> **Persistence should be expensive to abandon, not expensive to create.**

IPFS by itself enables content addressing and routing, but does not enforce long-term availability. Data disappears when it is no longer convenient to host.

IPFS-Sats introduces a jurisdictional gradient:

- Storing data is voluntary
- Maintaining persistence is rewarded continuously through the Lightning Yield Wallet
- Abandoning persistence is the higher-cost path — a host that stops serving blocks stops earning sats

No commands.
No censorship.
Only cost geometry.

---

## What IPFS-Sats Is

IPFS-Sats is a **Bitcoin-anchored commitment protocol** with three minimal functions:

**1. Commitment Anchoring**

Content is addressed by cryptographic hash — a Content Identifier (CID) derived from the hash of the content itself. A compact commitment — the Bundle Hash, a single 32-byte value representing the content and its governance rules combined — is anchored to Bitcoin using OP_RETURN. Bitcoin serves as the immutable provenance record of what is being committed to and when.

**2. Wealth-Based Persistence Pressure**

Satoshis are deployed into a Lightning Yield Wallet bonded to the content commitment. The LYW generates Lightning liquidity yield continuously — routing fees, channel leasing income — and that yield funds payments to the hosts serving the content. Persistence is maintained not by decree but because the economic incentive structure makes it the lower-cost path for every participant in the system. A host serving content earns. A host that stops serving earns nothing. The gradient enforces without commanding.

**3. Jurisdictional Enforcement Without Consensus Change**

Bitcoin consensus rules are untouched. No transaction filtering. No content judgment. No soft fork required. Enforcement occurs entirely through incentive gradients at the application layer. IPFS-Sats does not tell anyone what data to store. It makes promises expensive to break.

## What IPFS-Sats Is Not

- ❌ Not on-chain storage
- ❌ Not a Bitcoin fork, soft fork, or policy filter
- ❌ Not a content platform
- ❌ Not a cultural or expressive layer
- ❌ Not a replacement for IPFS
- ❌ Not a new token

IPFS-Sats does not try to make Bitcoin the archive.
It makes Bitcoin the **court of record**.

## On the On-Chain Data Debate

The Bitcoin community has been wrestling with a real question: what belongs in the timechain? Ordinals, inscriptions, OP_RETURN data, witness space optimization — these debates are not frivolous. They reflect genuine disagreement about what Bitcoin is for and what it should carry.

IPFS-Sats is not a position in that debate. It is a resolution to the underlying coordination failure that created the debate.

If off-chain persistence is unreliable, economically unsupported, and technically fragile — then storing data on-chain becomes the only credible option for anyone who needs their content to last. IPFS-Sats changes the cost surface. It makes off-chain persistence:

- Cheaper
- Stronger
- More expressive
- More economically honest
- Self-funding through Lightning yield

When off-chain persistence is as reliable as on-chain commitment, storing data on-chain is not prohibited — it simply becomes irrational. The debate resolves through economics, not through consensus changes, policy filters, or anyone deciding what belongs in a block.

Bitcoin gets to remain what it does best: verifying wealth and anchoring truth in time.

## Violations as Measurements

In jurisdictional systems, violations are not failures; they are **measurements of gradient strength**.

- Missing data
- Under-replication
- Slow retrieval
- Abandoned commitments

These outcomes reveal where persistence is underpriced, where enforcement is too permeable, where economic incentives have not yet reached. IPFS-Sats is designed to adjust through observation, not decree. Every gap in availability is a signal that the incentive gradient needs to be stronger — which means more sats deployed into LYWs, more hosts attracted by better pricing, more redundancy emerging from demand.

The system corrects itself. That is what jurisdictional enforcement through cost geometry means in practice.

---

## Why This Preserves Bitcoin's Integrity

IPFS-Sats resolves the tension created by the on-chain data debate without taking sides in it.

- Optional on-chain data remains valid
- No consensus splits
- No moral arbitration
- No cultural conflict imported into the Economic field
- No new token competing with Bitcoin's monetary network

Every payment in the system is denominated in sats. Every timestamp is a Bitcoin block. Every persistence guarantee is backed by Lightning liquidity. The protocol does not exist alongside Bitcoin — it is built from Bitcoin, denominated in Bitcoin, and anchored to Bitcoin at every layer.

The Bitcoiners who want the timechain protected get a credible off-chain alternative that actually works. The Bitcoiners who want to build on Bitcoin's monetary network get a protocol that routes every economic interaction through Lightning. The Bitcoiners who want open infrastructure that no one controls get a public good released with no company, no token, and no central authority. The Bitcoiners who want to see the exchange value of Bitcoin go up get an adoption curve accelerator — a killer application stack that creates real economic demand for sats through content monetization, LYW deployment, and micropayment flows at global scale, denominated in utility rather than speculation.

All of us.
